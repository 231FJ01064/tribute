Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

The Architect of the Indian Constitution

Biography

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, popularly known as Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, was an Indian jurist, social reformer, and the principal architect of the Constitution of India. He was born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh, India. He was a champion of the rights of the marginalized and fought against the caste system and untouchability in India. His work and legacy continue to inspire millions around the world, and he is regarded as one of the most significant social reformers of the 20th century.

Achievements

  • Principal architect of the Indian Constitution.
  • First Law Minister of Independent India.
  • Leader of the Dalit Buddhist movement.
  • Fought for social justice, equality, and the abolition of untouchability.
  • Received the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, posthumously in 1990.
